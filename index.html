SERVICES

  • Polish :
    1. Glaze and Wax Polish:
      A combination of a mild abrasive to remove minor defects and fillers to mask imperfections, followed by a wax element to protect the finish. This type of polish may not last as long as other types.
    2. Compound Polish:
      A stronger and more abrasive type of polish, used to remove heavy scratches and imperfections. It may require a subsequent polishing and waxing step to achieve a high-gloss finish.
    3. All-in-One Polish:
      A single product that combines the functions of a polish, wax, and sealant. These products are often easy to use and provide a high-gloss finish.
    4. Ceramic Polish:
      A type of polish that uses ceramic technology to provide a durable, long-lasting finish. These products often include a sealant and can be used to protect the paint from the elements.
    5. Hybrid Polish:
      A combination of different polishing agents and technologies, often used to provide a high-gloss finish and protection for the paint.
    6. Paste Polish:
      A type of polish that comes in a paste form, often used for more aggressive polishing and correction of heavy scratches and imperfections.
    7. Liquid Polish:
      A type of polish that comes in a liquid form, often used for general polishing and detailing.
    8. Spray Polish:
      A type of polish that comes in a spray form, often used for quick and easy polishing and detailing.
  • Teflon Putting
    Teflon coating is applied as a transparent protective layer on top of your car’s paintwork. It is made of a thin layer of polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE), a synthetic fluoropolymer of tetrafluoroethylene. This non-stick coating forms a protective shield over the car’s paintwork, guarding it against environmental elements such as UV rays, acid rain, bird droppings, and pollutants.
  • Ceramic Coating
    A type of coating that is hydrophobic, meaning it repels water and other liquids. It is also known for its ability to provide long-lasting protection against the elements and daily wear and tear.
  • Interior clean
    the process of thoroughly cleaning and maintaining the interior of a vehicle, including all surfaces, crevices, and compartments. This includes cleaning the seats, carpets, dashboard, windows, and other interior components to remove dirt, dust, stains.
